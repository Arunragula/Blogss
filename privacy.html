Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 13, 2026

This policy explains what data DotComDaily collects and how it's used. The short version: this site collects the minimum needed to run comments, reactions, and the newsletter — no ad trackers, no data sold to third parties.

1. What we collect

  • Newsletter: your email address, if you subscribe.
  • Comments: the name and message you choose to submit on a post.

2. Cookies and tracking

This site does not use advertising or cross-site tracking cookies. Browser local storage is used only for the site's own functionality, described above.

3. Third-party links

Posts and the Products page may link to external sites, including affiliate links. DotComDaily isn't responsible for the privacy practices of those third-party sites.

4. Your choices

You can unsubscribe from the newsletter at any time (a link is included in every email), and you can clear your browser's local storage to remove locally stored data at any time.

5. Children's privacy

This site is not directed at children under 13, and does not knowingly collect data from them.

6. Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated occasionally; the "last updated" date above will always reflect the latest version.

7. Contact

Questions about this policy? Reach out via the contact page.
